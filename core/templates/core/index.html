Support Beirut

This past month has seen dramatic events unfold in our beloved country. In less than ten seconds, more than 250.000 Lebanese lost their houses, and more than 750.000 Lebanese found themselves living in their houses without windows, balconies, doors, furniture, and fixtures. We are sure that your sentiments are echoed in the hope for a new leaf to be turned in the coming years with a brighter future. A future that brings with it stability, peace, and a turnaround for our economy, our governance, and, most importantly, our people. Although the road ahead of us will be long and painful, we should continue to stand together and extend our support in any way we can, as we aspire for a brighter future. Questscope is an international non-governmental, non-profit organization founded in 1988 to put the last, first. We rely on the theory of social change that recognizes the interconnected nature of individual transformation, institutional response, and policy changed at the governance level. The horrific blast has brought into sharp focus the need for the international community to step up and help Lebanon and its people. That is way Questscope is devoted to helping people in Beirut city, especially the ones whose homes were affected by the blast. We aim to restore and help rebuild houses for people who cannot afford to rebuild or repair their houses at their own expense.
